4. Storage and Data Security

Storage Location: We differentiate between two types of data storage based on the nature of the data:

  • Customer Master Data (Contract and Account Information): All data relating to your subscription, billing, and company identity (as listed in Section 2.1) is stored and processed securely within the European Union (EU).
  • Platform and Processing Data (Usage, Order, and Machine Data): The data processed through our iPaaS platform (as listed in Sections 2.2 and 2.3) is stored in the geographical region selected by the customer. Our product can be hosted in the EU, or within the North America region (specifically the United States or Canada), or the Asia region (specifically Singapore), depending on your choice. We will inform the customer of the exact hosting country during the setup process.

International Data Transfers

For hosting locations outside the EU (North America and Asia), we ensure an adequate level of data protection in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). To further safeguard data transferred outside the EU, we implement additional technical and organizational measures (TOMs) such as encryption and pseudonymization, alongside standard contractual clauses (SCCs) or other legally recognized mechanisms.

Storage Duration: We store your data only for as long as necessary for the performance of the contract and compliance with legal retention obligations.

After termination of the contractual relationship, we generally retain your data for 3 months for potential reactivation. After that, all data will be deleted, unless you request longer retention or legal obligations prevent deletion.

5. Your Rights as a Data Subject (GDPR)

You have the right to assert the following rights at any time:

  • Right of Access (Art. 15 GDPR): You can request confirmation as to whether we process personal data concerning you.
  • Right to Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You can request the immediate rectification of inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You can request the deletion of your data, provided there is no legal obligation for retention.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18 GDPR): You can request the restriction of processing.
  • Right to Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR): You have the right to receive the data concerning you in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object (Art. 21 GDPR): You can object to the processing of your data that is carried out based on our legitimate interest.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint (Art. 77 GDPR): You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (e.g., the Information Commissioner's Office in the United Kingdom).
